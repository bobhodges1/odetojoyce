James Joyce

The greatest author of the twentieth century

Here's a time line of James Joyce's life:

  • 1882 - Joyce is born in Rathgar, Dublin
  • 1899 - Begins attending University College, Dublin
  • 1900 - Publishes ‘Ibsen’s New Drama’
  • 1901 - ‘The Day of the Rabblement’ is published, his first.
  • 1902 - Joyce makes his first trip to Paris
  • 1904 - Marries his wife, Nora, and shortly after movies to Trieste
  • 1911 - Starts giving lectures on Shakespeare in Trieste
  • 1914 - Dubliners is published; A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man is serialized in The Egoist. Moves to Zurich shortly after
  • 1918 - The serialization of Ulysses in The Little Review begins
  • 1921 - Ulysses is put on trial for obscenity and banned in USA
  • 1922 - After moving back to Paris, Uylsses is published elsewhere
  • 1933 - New case is heard, United States v. One Book Called Ulysses. Finally USA can publish the best book of the century
  • 1939 - Despite being largely blind, Finnegans Wake is published. Spends his last years helping Jewish people escape Nazi persecution
  • 1941 - Passes away aged 58 in Zurich
